# The Complete Guide to Property Verification in Africa

Buying property in Africa from abroad comes with unique challenges and risks. This guide walks you through everything you need to know about verifying property before you invest.

## Why Property Verification Matters

Real estate fraud is a significant concern across many African markets. Without proper verification, you risk losing your investment to fraudulent sellers, disputed ownership, or invalid documentation.

## Key Steps in Property Verification

### 1. Title Search

A title search confirms that the seller has legal ownership of the property and that there are no existing claims or liens against it.

### 2. Land Registry Check

Visit the local land registry to verify all documentation is authentic and properly registered with government authorities.

### 3. Physical Inspection

A physical site inspection confirms the property exists as described and helps identify any encroachments or boundary issues.

### 4. Neighbor Verification

Speaking with neighbors can reveal important information about the property's history and any disputes.

### 5. Document Review

Review all sale agreements, title deeds, and permits with qualified legal professionals.

## Common Red Flags

- Seller unable to produce original documents
- Prices significantly below market value
- Pressure to complete the sale quickly
- Unwillingness to allow professional inspections
- Discrepancies in property descriptions
