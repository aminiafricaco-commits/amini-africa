Relocating to Nairobi can be an exciting but overwhelming experience. Whether you are a returning diaspora professional or an expatriate taking up a new role, proper planning makes all the difference.

## Finding the Right Neighbourhood

Nairobi offers diverse residential areas to suit different lifestyles and budgets. Popular choices include Karen for families seeking space and greenery, Westlands for urban professionals, and Runda for executive living.

## Schools and Education

Nairobi has excellent international and local schools. The Kenya National Curriculum, IGCSE, IB, and American systems are all available. We recommend starting school research at least three months before your move.

## Banking and Finance

Opening a bank account as a returnee has become simpler. Most major banks offer diaspora accounts with preferential rates. Requirements typically include a passport, KRA PIN, and proof of address.

## Healthcare

Nairobi has world-class hospitals and clinics. We recommend securing health insurance before arrival — several providers offer diaspora-specific plans.
