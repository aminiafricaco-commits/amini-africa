# Procurement Due Diligence: How to Verify African Suppliers

Sourcing products from Africa offers tremendous opportunities, but finding reliable suppliers requires thorough due diligence.

## Why Supplier Verification Matters

Working with unverified suppliers can lead to quality issues, delivery delays, and financial losses. Proper verification protects your business and reputation.

## Essential Verification Steps

### Company Registration Check

Verify the supplier is properly registered with relevant government authorities and has legal standing to operate.

### Physical Factory Visit

Visit the supplier's facilities to confirm they have the capacity and capability to fulfill your orders.

### Reference Checks

Speak with other clients who have worked with the supplier to verify their track record.

### Quality Assessment

Inspect product samples and assess quality control processes.

### Financial Verification

Confirm the supplier's financial stability through available records.

## Warning Signs

- No verifiable physical address
- Refusal to allow factory visits
- Demanding full payment upfront
- Inconsistent communication
- Unrealistic pricing or promises
