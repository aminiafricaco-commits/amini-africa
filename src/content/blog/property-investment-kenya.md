Kenya remains one of the most attractive property markets in Sub-Saharan Africa for diaspora investors. With a growing economy, stable legal framework, and increasing demand for housing, the opportunities are significant. However, investing from abroad comes with unique challenges.

## Understanding the Legal Landscape

Foreigners and diaspora Kenyans can own property in Kenya, but there are important distinctions. Kenyan citizens living abroad retain full property rights, while foreign investors face certain restrictions under the Land Control Act.

## Due Diligence is Critical

Before purchasing any property, ensure:

- Title search at the Ministry of Lands
- Verification of land rates and rent clearance
- Physical site inspection
- Confirmation of zoning and development approvals

## Common Pitfalls

The most common issues we encounter include double allocations, fraudulent title deeds, and disputes over boundaries. Having a trusted representative on the ground mitigates these risks significantly.
